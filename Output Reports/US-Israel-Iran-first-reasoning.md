## How This Works

Each evidence item is scored against four hypotheses using a 7-level weight scale:

| Weight |                   Meaning                                |
|--------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| HIGH_SUPPORT   |  Strongly consistent — significant upward update |
| MEDIUM_SUPPORT |     Consistent — moderate upward update          |
| LOW_SUPPORT    |                 Weakly consistent                |
| NA             |       No relevance to this hypothesis            |
| LOW_REFUTE     |               Weakly inconsistent                |
| MEDIUM_REFUTE  |   Inconsistent — moderate downward update        |
| HIGH_REFUTE | Strongly inconsistent — significant downward update |

**Key principle:** Following Heuer's ACH philosophy, the analyst looks for *inconsistency*, not confirmation. The hypothesis that survives the most evidence without contradiction is the working hypothesis — not the one with the most supporting evidence.
