## What This Report Is

This is the first complete report generated by the Bayesian ACH engine. It assesses the most likely near-term outcome of the 2026 US-Iran War using structured analytic methodology — not opinion or intuition.

**ACH works differently from conventional analysis.** Most analysis asks: *what supports my hypothesis?* ACH asks: *what is inconsistent with each hypothesis?* The hypothesis that survives the most evidence without direct contradiction becomes the working hypothesis. This directly counters confirmation bias, the single most documented failure mode in intelligence analysis (Heuer, 1999).

## Key Judgment

> **H3 (Iranian Capitulation) is the least inconsistent outcome given current evidence — not because Iran is capitulating, but because ceasefire, ground invasion, and stalemate face stronger contradictions in the evidence set.**

This is an ACH elimination judgment, not a prediction. The engine identifies which hypothesis survives the evidence most intact not which outcome is most likely in the real world.

---

## Why Each Hypothesis Moved

### H1: Negotiated Ceasefire — 3.6% (↓↓↓)
Heavily undermined by multiple strong contradictions:
- Iran's formal "heavy price" vow directly contradicts ceasefire readiness
- Trump's "begging me to be Supreme Leader" framing signals no serious diplomatic framework exists
- Gulf states actively pushing *against* a simple ceasefire, demanding capability degradation instead
- Witkoff expecting talks provides weak support but insufficient to overcome accumulated contradictions

### H2: Ground Invasion — 5.5% (↓↓↓)
Undermined primarily by diplomatic signals:
- Pentagon readying troops is preparatory, not decisive *readying ≠ deploying*
- Deadline extensions and Witkoff's talk expectations reduce immediate escalation pressure
- Trump issuing demands verbally rather than militarily contradicts ground invasion signal
- Gulf states want degradation but this doesn't exclusively mean ground war

### H3: Iran Capitulates — 70.4% (↑↑↑)
**Least inconsistent by elimination — not confirmed:**
- Most scored evidence refutes H1, H2, H4 more strongly than it refutes H3
- Iran allowing 10 tankers through Hormuz is calibrated bargaining, not capitulation but doesn't directly contradict H3 either
- Trump's public demands (Hormuz, 15-point plan) create a framework under which capitulation is theoretically possible
- **Critical caveat:** Prior knowledge strongly suggests Iran views its nuclear program as non-negotiable this was deliberately excluded from evidence weights (Heuer discipline) but would significantly reduce H3 if modeled

### H4: Protracted Stalemate — 20.5% (↓)
Undermined by active multi-party pressure:
- Gulf states demanding active capability degradation contradicts passive deadlock
- Trump deadlines and 15-point plan signal movement, not stasis
- Multiple active mediators (Pakistan, Oman) engaged
- Ongoing aerial strikes *without* resolution partially supports H4, keeping it above H1 and H2

## Caveats & Limitations

1. **Partial scoring:** ~11 of ~150 fetched articles manually scored. Full scoring would produce more robust output
2. **Prior knowledge excluded from weights:** Deliberately following Heuer's discipline analyst background knowledge kept out of individual evidence weights, reserved for summary judgment
3. **News is 1 day old:** Fast-moving conflict, assessments shift rapidly
4. **Single analyst, no red team:** No alternative hypothesis generation or adversarial review
5. **English-language bias:** GDELT sources skew Western, Iranian perspective underrepresented
6. **ACH ≠ prediction:** The engine identifies the least inconsistent hypothesis, not the most likely real-world outcome
